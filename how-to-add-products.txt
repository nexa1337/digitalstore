HOW TO ADD PRODUCTS TO YOUR STORE
=================================

Your store is connected to a Google Sheet. To add, edit, or remove products, you simply need to update the Google Sheet. The website will automatically fetch the latest data!

STEP 1: OPEN YOUR GOOGLE SHEET
------------------------------
Open the Google Sheet that is linked to your website. (This is the URL you provided in the VITE_GOOGLE_SHEET_CSV_URL environment variable).

STEP 2: ADD A NEW ROW
---------------------
Scroll to the bottom of the sheet and add a new row for your product. 

STEP 3: FILL OUT THE COLUMNS
----------------------------
Make sure your Google Sheet has the following columns (the exact spelling matters):

id (e.g., "1", "2", "my-script")
title (e.g., "Ultimate UI Kit")
priceUSD (e.g., "49.00")
shortDescription (e.g., "A great UI kit.")
fullDescription (You can use Markdown here, including ![alt](image-url.gif) for GIFs)
category (e.g., "Code", "Design")
type (e.g., "Figma File", "React Source Code")
images (Comma-separated URLs, e.g., https://image1.jpg, https://image2.gif)
features (Comma-separated list, e.g., Responsive, Dark Mode, Free Updates)
gumroadLink (e.g., https://gumroad.com/l/your-product)
size (e.g., "1.2 GB" or "45 MB")
pages (e.g., "120" or "0")
livePreviewUrl (Optional: e.g., https://nexa1337.com)

STEP 4: FORMATTING YOUR "fullDescription"
-----------------------------------------
How to format your "fullDescription" in Google Sheets:
Subtitles: Use hashtags (e.g., ## My Subtitle or ### Smaller Subtitle)
Bold Text: Wrap text in double asterisks (e.g., **This is bold**)
Lists: Use dashes or numbers (e.g., - First item or 1. First item)
Images: Use markdown image syntax (e.g., ![Image Description](https://your-image-link.jpg)) or standard HTML (<img src="https://your-image-link.jpg" />)

STEP 5: PUBLISH / SAVE
----------------------
Google Sheets saves automatically! As long as your sheet is published to the web (File > Share > Publish to web -> as CSV), your website will instantly reflect the new products the next time you refresh the page.
